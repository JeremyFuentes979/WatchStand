**Date:** 2026-07-14
**Status:** Draft
**Author:** WatchStand Developer Team

---

## 1. Executive Summary

WatchStand is a free, private, zero-account wellness toolkit for first responders. The most effective path to adoption is through **Critical Incident Stress Management (CISM) teams** — the trusted peer-support infrastructure already embedded in police, fire, and EMS agencies. This document outlines a step-by-step outreach strategy to connect with CISM organizations, demonstrate WatchStand's value, and get the tool into the hands of the people who need it.

**Goal:** Establish WatchStand as the recommended wellness resource for CISM teams nationwide, building a grassroots network of departments that trust and use the tool.

## 2. Key CISM Organizations

### National / International

| Organization | Description | Relevance to WatchStand |
|---|---|---|
| **ICISF** (International Critical Incident Stress Foundation) | The founding and largest CISM organization. Sets standards of care, offers training/certification, maintains a directory of registered CISM teams. | Primary partner. ICISF-affiliated teams are the most structured and organized. Can present WatchStand at ICISF conferences and in their resource library. |
| **National Volunteer Fire Council (NVFC) — Share the Load Program** | Fire/EMS-specific behavioral health program. Offers peer support training, resources, and the Fire/EMS Helpline (888-731-3473). | Natural fit. NVFC's network of fire/EMS departments is a direct channel for WatchStand deployment. |
| **COPLINE** | 24/7 hotline for law enforcement staffed by retired officers. | Peer-to-peer model aligns with WatchStand's anonymous design. Can cross-reference as a crisis resource. |
| **First Responder Support Network (FRSN)** | Offers peer support, education, and resources for first responders and families. | Potential partner for distributing WatchStand wellness tools at their training events. |
| **Safe Call Now** | Confidential crisis hotline for public safety employees and their families. | 24/7 resource that pairs naturally with WatchStand's crisis directory. |

### State & Regional CISM Teams

Most states have:
- **State CISM Coordinator** — appointed by the state emergency management or health department
- **Regional CISM Teams** — organized by county or EMS region
- **Department-level Peer Support Teams** — within larger police/fire departments

**Key targets for initial outreach:**
- State CISM coordinators (list available via ICISF state affiliate network)
- Large metro police/fire department peer support coordinators
- State police/Highway patrol wellness programs
- State firefighter association wellness committees

## 3. How Departments Connect with CISM Teams

### The Typical Flow

1. **Incident occurs** — critical incident (line-of-duty death, mass casualty, officer-involved shooting, etc.)
2. **Department requests CISM support** — via ICISF-affiliated team, state coordinator, or regional team
3. **CISM team deploys** — provides on-scene peer support, group defusings, and follow-up
4. **Ongoing support** — CISM team may recommend resources for continued wellness

### Where WatchStand Fits

WatchStand is the **always-available, always-anonymous** resource that CISM teams can recommend during and after a deployment:
- **During a callout** — officers can use grounding and breathing tools immediately
- **Post-incident** — decompression protocols and mood check-ins provide structured support between CISM visits
- **Long-term** — crisis hotline directory and cognitive reframe tools are available 24/7 without any barrier to access

### The Value Proposition for CISM Teams

CISM teams are often stretched thin. They can't be everywhere at once. WatchStand gives them a force multiplier — a tool they can confidently recommend that:
- Requires **zero training** to use
- Requires **zero IT approval** to deploy (it's a web link)
- **Collects no data** — no liability for the department or CISM team
- Is **always available** — 24/7, even when no CISM team member is on call

## 4. Step-by-Step Outreach Plan

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

**Goal:** Build relationships with key national organizations and establish WatchStand as a known resource.

| Step | Action | Owner | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Join ICISF as a member (individual or organizational) | Lead | Membership confirmed |
| 1.2 | Create an ICISF-affiliated CISM team listing for WatchStand | Lead | Team registered with ICISF |
| 1.3 | Contact ICISF about presenting at their annual conference or webinar series | Lead | Scheduled presentation or webinar |
| 1.4 | Submit WatchStand to the ICISF resource library | Lead | Resource listed on ICISF site |
| 1.5 | Reach out to NVFC Share the Load program — introduce WatchStand as a free tool for fire/EMS | Lead | Positive response, potential collaboration |
| 1.6 | Prepare a one-page "CISM Team Deploy Guide" for WatchStand | Developer | PDF created, posted on site |

### Phase 2: Regional Outreach (Weeks 5–8)

**Goal:** Connect with state CISM coordinators and regional teams to get WatchStand deployed.

| Step | Action | Owner | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | Compile list of state CISM coordinators (via ICISF state affiliate network) | Lead | List complete |
| 2.2 | Email each coordinator with a personalized introduction to WatchStand | Lead | Emails sent, follow-ups scheduled |
| 2.3 | Offer a 15-minute virtual demo to interested coordinators | Lead | 5+ demos conducted |
| 2.4 | Create a "Department Deployment Kit" — a single-page PDF with a private link, privacy guarantees, and setup instructions | Developer | Kit created |
| 2.5 | Identify 10 target departments (mix of police, fire, EMS) in regions with engaged CISM coordinators | Lead | List identified |
| 2.6 | Offer free deployment to those 10 departments, with support | Lead | 5+ departments deployed |

### Phase 3: Growth & Network Effects (Weeks 9–12)

**Goal:** Scale through word-of-mouth, testimonials, and conference presence.

| Step | Action | Owner | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Collect anonymous usage stories from early-adopter departments (with permission) | Lead | 3+ testimonials |
| 3.2 | Present at ICISF annual conference or regional CISM training events | Lead | Presentation delivered |
| 3.3 | Create a "CISM Teams" page on the WatchStand site — a dedicated page explaining how CISM teams can use and recommend WatchStand | Developer | Page live |
| 3.4 | Develop a referral program: CISM teams that recommend WatchStand get a dedicated dashboard showing anonymized aggregate trends | Developer | Feature built |
| 3.5 | Follow up with all contacted coordinators and departments — offer support, gather feedback | Lead | Feedback documented |

## 9. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| CISM teams are skeptical of new tools | Medium | High | Focus on privacy guarantees and zero-data model. Lead with "no account, no data, no catch." |
| Slow adoption by departments | Medium | Medium | Start with CISM team endorsements. Departments trust CISM teams more than vendors. |
| ICISF partnership takes time | Medium | Low | Pursue multiple channels simultaneously (NVFC, state coordinators, regional teams). |
| No budget for conferences | High | Medium | Use virtual presentations and webinars. Record demos. Focus on email and phone outreach. |
| Competing with existing wellness programs | Low | Medium | Emphasize that WatchStand complements existing programs — it's the anonymous front door that leads officers to seek more help. |
